            Important note

            How this version works

            This section now uses the same grouped, tiered card language as the results so the page feels like one coherent system rather than two cousins who met at Christmas.

            Summary

            The tool starts by giving you the short version first, because not every moment of life is a suitable time for a nutritional dissertation.

            First read

            Fast pattern summary

            • It identifies likely food matches from your text and builds a rough picture of what showed up.
            • It surfaces the headline read first so you can get the gist without wading through every detail card.
            • It uses plain wording as well as colour so good signs, caution points, and uncertainty are named directly.

            Analysis

            Once the quick read is out of the way, the tool shows how it reached that conclusion.

            Pattern scoring

            What it checks

            • It scores likely protein, fibre, iron, calcium, fruit and vegetable coverage, and steadiness of energy support.
            • It separates good signs from caution points so the result does not blur into one giant beige nutrition paragraph.
            • It groups related findings together, which makes the long scroll easier to scan and easier to trust.

            Guidance

            After the analysis, it shifts from description into practical support.

            Useful next steps

            Why the advice sounds this way

            • It injects short guidance summaries tied to Australian nutrition resources instead of just dumping a link and wandering off.
            • It offers practical upgrades that aim for realistic improvements, not fantasy-perfect meal plans.
            • It keeps the tone direct and usable, which is more helpful than sounding like a brochure in activewear.

            Meta

            This is the honesty layer. It tells you what the tool recognised and where it might be guessing.

            Keep your scepticism switched on

            Transparency and limits

            • It stays transparent about uncertainty rather than pretending your vague pizza memory came with a laboratory report.
            • It shows recognised foods and confidence cues so you can judge whether the output feels grounded.
            • It is still heuristic, not clinical. Its job is to be useful, not theatrical.

            What this experiment demonstrates

            This prototype is designed as a portfolio piece. It shows how structured content design, rules-based logic, and accessible interaction can work together in a practical tool.

            Accessible information architecture

            The interface is structured to support predictable navigation, progressive disclosure, and clear reading order.

            Structure and navigation

            How the interaction model is designed

            • The page separates input, results, explanation, and portfolio context into distinct sections.
            • Results are grouped into expandable sections so users can move through the output in manageable chunks.
            • Status updates, headings, controls, and visibility changes are intentionally structured rather than left to default browser behaviour.

            Rules-based content interpretation

            The tool translates messy everyday meal descriptions into usable guidance through explicit logic rather than opaque automation.

            Reasoning model

            What the underlying logic demonstrates

            • Free-text input is matched against a defined food dataset using recognisable patterns and keywords.
            • Recognised foods contribute to nutrient and pattern scoring across categories such as protein, fibre, iron, calcium, and meal balance.
            • The output remains explainable because each conclusion is tied to visible rules and recognised inputs.

            Practical guidance design

            The prototype focuses on turning analysis into realistic next steps rather than producing generic health advice.

            Useful output

            How the guidance layer works

            • The response moves from quick read to deeper detail so the most useful information arrives first.
            • Suggestions are shaped by context options such as low effort, sensory tolerance, grazing patterns, and vegetarian preferences.
            • The tone is direct and usable, supporting comprehension without sounding clinical or performative.

            Transparency and trust signals

            The prototype makes its own limitations visible, which is a core part of the design rather than an afterthought.

            Trust and boundaries

            How uncertainty is communicated

            • Recognised foods are surfaced so users can judge whether the interpretation is accurate.
            • Confidence and caution notes are included to show where the system is making rough inferences.
            • The tool is framed as guidance for everyday decision-making, not diagnosis or personalised medical advice.

            Feed Me Properly is a work in progress.

            It is for general nutrition guidance and practical next-step thinking, not diagnosis or personalised medical care.
