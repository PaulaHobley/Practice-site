Experiments

This is where the half-tamed ideas live. Some are already useful. Some are still finding their feet. All of them are part of the process.

What this page is for

This page gathers the projects, tools, and test builds that sit somewhere between first draft and properly battle-tested. It exists so I can try things, refine them, and learn by building rather than waiting for some mythical moment when everything is magically finished.

The important bit: nothing here is being presented as flawless. These experiments are works in progress. They will evolve over time, improve as I learn more, and occasionally behave like tiny chaos goblins before being brought back into line.

What to expect

  • Accessible structure and plain language from the start, even when the feature itself is still being refined.
  • Experiments that are practical first and polished second.
  • Updates over time as I test ideas, fix rough edges, and make things more robust.
  • The odd imperfect corner, because that is how learning by doing actually works.

Current experiments

  • Tools hub

    A landing point for accessible mini-tools and interactive bits that are being built, tested, and steadily sharpened up.

    Status: active, useful, and still evolving.

  • Snark Generator

    A rules-based response generator for guide dog etiquette violations. It aims to be accessible, a little educational, and not remotely interested in pretending nonsense should go unanswered.

    Status: functional, intentionally opinionated, and subject to future tweaks.

  • Feed Me Properly

    A nutrition-focused experiment built around practical guidance and Australian context. It is designed to be helpful now while leaving room for smarter rules and deeper resource support later.

    Status: growing steadily and very much not the final form.

Why I am leaving the rough edges visible

Because pretending things arrive perfect is rubbish. This section is meant to show the actual process: testing an idea, working out what holds up, fixing what does not, and improving the accessibility and usability as the project matures.

In other words, this is less museum display and more workshop bench. A bit of sawdust is to be expected.

Ongoing note

If something here feels unfinished, that is probably because it is. The plan is not perfection on day one. The plan is to keep building, keep testing, and keep making the experiments stronger over time.

Experimental Web Playground is a working space, which is a tidy way of saying the experiments may keep mutating into better versions of themselves.
